Festivals of Kerala

The diverse south Indian state of Kerala can also be called a 'land of festivals', because of long list of fairs and festivals that are celebrated here all round the year. However, the dates of the festivals change every year as per the Malayalam calendar. Every festival has its own importance and is celebrated with great zeal by the people of all the castes and religions. During the celebrations, the classical music, dance and other traditional rituals comes alive. People clean and decorate their homes, exchange gifts, wear new clothes, relish elaborate meals along with the loved ones, offer prayers to their deities and partake in processions, which are great shows of rhythm and culture. Elephants are also an important part of Kerala's festivities. Following are the main festivals in Kerala:

Onam Festival

The yearly harvest festival, Onam, is a 10-day event that starts on Attam and is concluded on Thiruvonam. Kerala's most important festival is rejoiced to welcome the spirit of King Mahabali.

Navarathri Festival

Navarathri is one of the most prominent Hindu festivals celebrated in September-October. During the festival, the 9 incarnations of Goddess Durga are worshipped.

Thrissur Pooram Festival

Celebrated in April-May, Thrissur Pooram is a major festival held in Thrissur. The highlight is the procession that includes 101 bedecked elephants.

Pongala Festival

Pongala is organized at Attukal Bhagavathy temple, where female devotees prepare Pongala, a sweet porridge, in clay pots over an open fire as an offering.

Kerala Village Fair

The Kerala Village Fair, held in January, recreates the traditional village life of Kerala, featuring handlooms, handicrafts, and cultural performances.

Aluva Festival

Celebrated in February-March, Aluva Sivarathri is held on the banks of the Periyar River, remembering the event when Lord Shiva consumed poison to save the world.

Makaravilakku Festival

Makaravilakku, held at Sabarimala in January, is attended by thousands of devotees to offer prayers to Lord Ayyappa.

Easter Festival

Easter is celebrated in April to commemorate the resurrection of Christ, with prayers and festivities in Christian communities across Kerala.

Vishu Festival

Vishu marks the astronomical New Year for Hindus in Kerala. Kani Kanal, a ceremonial viewing of auspicious objects, is performed for prosperity.
